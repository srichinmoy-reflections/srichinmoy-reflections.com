OCTOBER 3RD

 

1975
    Sri Chinmoy inscribed his 100,000th painting with the soulful dedication:

“God-Compassion, Earth-Aspiration
God-Vision, Man-Reality
— Perfection —”

  

Sri Chinmoy completed his 100,000th Jharna-Kala painting in Jamaica, NY, USA. From November 19, 1974, when he first began painting, it took just 10½ months for him to reach his goal. During the final 10 days of his painting marathon he barely slept, completing as many as 5,000 works per day. Read more…

1980
    “I had not realised that somewhere tucked away in this very large building of the United Nations there is a small corner where real peace dwells. Along with the large number of Ambassadors, members of the diplomatic corps and others who are representing India here, Sri Chinmoy is also doing some work which is even more important, because people like him are our cultural and spiritual Ambassadors. In many respects the work which I am doing and the work which he is doing are supplementary and complementary. I once again wish you greater and greater triumph in this very sacred task which you are doing.” — Narasimha Rao, Indian Minister of External Affairs (later Prime Minister of India)  

Sri Chinmoy met with Narasimha Rao, Minister of External Affairs of India, at a special programme hosted in his honour by Sri Chinmoy Meditation at the United Nations in the Dag Hammarskjöld Auditorium at the United Nations in New York. As part of the function Sri Chinmoy’s song ‘Brother Narasimha Rao!’ was performed by the meditation group choir.

    Referring to Sri Chinmoy’s students: “Like these people, I too see something in you.... Thank you very much. It is a great honour that you are doing this for me.” — Lillian Carter  

Sri Chinmoy met with Lillian Carter, mother of President Jimmy Carter, in Americus, GA, USA. Sri Chinmoy sang her the song ‘Compassion-Ocean, Mother Lillian’, which he had composed the day before. Later that evening Sri Chinmoy and his students gave a public concert in her honour.

 

1980

Sri Chinmoy launched the Salutation to America 1,300-mile non-stop relay at midnight in Concord, NH, USA. The relay, comprised of 15 runners from the Sri Chinmoy Marathon Team, went on to carry a flaming torch through each of the 13 original states of the US and completed its journey seven days later in Atlanta, Georgia.
