AUGUST 8TH

 

1933
    “My parents and my elder sisters and brothers brought me to the Ashram and the spiritual life – the place of my heart’s awakening and my life’s oneness-realisation with the highest Absolute Supreme. Nothing can be more important, more significant, more meaningful and more fruitful in the life of a seeker than to go and sit at the feet of one’s Master.” — Sri Chinmoy, August 8, 1988  

As a young child, Madal (Chinmoy) was brought to the Sri Aurobindo Ashram in Pondicherry, India, by his parents for the first time for a short visit. At just under 2 years of age, he travelled with his family on the four-day steam train journey from Chittagong in East Bengal. The family undertook three more round trips to the Ashram in 1936, 1939 and 1941 – each time covering the arduous, 5,000-km journey – before all children finally became permanent members of the Ashram after both parents passed away in 1944.
