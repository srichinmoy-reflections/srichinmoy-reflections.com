  NOVEMBER 1ST


1969

Sri Chinmoy delivered a lecture, ‘The Body’s Reality And The Soul's Reality’, at Siliman University, Dumaguete, in the Philippines.


1975
    “In our inner life we always seek to transcend our previous realisations and achievements while we are walking along Eternity’s Road of perfect Perfection.” — Sri Chinmoy  


Sri Chinmoy completed writing 843 poems in 24 hours (from midnight October 31st to midnight November 1st.), in Jamaica, NY, USA. The book of these poems, entitled Transcendence-Perfection was published the next day by Agni Press.


1977
       


The open-air billboard exhibition of Sri Chinmoy’s 13’ x 25’ Jharna-Kala painting ‘Journey’s Battle Victory’, commissioned by The Eyes and Ears Foundation in conjunction with San Francisco’s Museum of Modern Art, opened at the Embarcadero in San Francisco, CA, USA.


1983

Sri Chinmoy began writing rhyming poems, published as Silence Speaks, in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1986
       


Sri Chinmoy achieved a 705¾-lb. lift, using only his right arm, in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1986

Sri Chinmoy lifted 26 marathon runners (total weight of 4,328 lbs.); and lifted a steamroller (1,640 lbs.) in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1990

Sri Chinmoy lifted Constantine Moushoutas, former Permanent Representative of Cyprus to the UN; Lamuel Stanislaus, former Permanent Representative of Grenada to the UN; and Winston Thompson, Permanent Representative of Fiji to the UN, in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1995

Sri Chinmoy offered a Peace Concert at Public School 86 in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1996
       


Sri Chinmoy offered a Peace Concert at Corestates Spectrum to an audience of 10,500 in Philadelphia, PA, USA.


1999

Papua New Guinea was declared a Sri Chinmoy Peace-Blossom-Nation.


2000

Sri Chinmoy offered a Peace Concert and delivered a lecture in honour of Sri Aurobindo. Also received a Proclamation of Honour at King’s College, University of Cambridge in Cambridge, UK.

 

NOVEMBER 2ND


1988

Sri Chinmoy lifted Chidambaram Subramaniam, President of Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan, former Governor of Maharashtra; and Sundaram Ramakrishnan, Executive Secretary and Director-General of Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan, in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1988

Sri Chinmoy lifted 18 people in Jamaica, NY, USA.


1993
    From a letter dated October 29, 1993: “A meeting with Your paintings is a Meeting with the Nobility of the Human Soul.” — Raisa Maximovna Gorbacheva  


An exhibition of 100,000 of Sri Chinmoy's small bird drawings, entitled ‘Dream-Freedom Peace-Birds’, opened at the Heritage Building in Ottawa, ON, Canada. The exhibit was dedicated to Raisa Maximovna Gorbacheva, wife of former Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev.


1998

Sri Chinmoy lifted a Piper Arrow III plane plus eight men – a total weight of 4,748 lbs. – at the Sparta Aviation School, Teterboro, New Jersey, USA. After the lift, Sri Chinmoy was interviewed by The Record newspaper, Bergen, New Jersey.


2000

The Republic of the Marshall Islands was declared a Sri Chinmoy Peace-Blossom-Nation.


2002

Sri Chinmoy lifted trees, motorcycles, construction equipment and a helicopter in New Jersey environs, USA.


2005
    “I entirely endorse the idea which was started by Sri Chinmoy: to create a more harmonious and a more friendly world for all, through running together for world harmony.” — Paul Tergat, Marathon World Record Holder  


Sri Chinmoy lifted Paul Tergat, marathon world-record holder, 1996 and 2000 Olympic silver medallist, in Jamaica, NY, USA.

Thomas, Permanent Representative of Liberia to the United Nations

NOVEMBER 6TH


1986
    “I am especially blessed by this recognition [the U Thant Peace Award], for it is given by a great man of peace — Sri Chinmoy — in the name of another great man of peace — U Thant. It was the commitment to the universal spirit that connects all people to the light and love of the Supreme that made U Thant a great man of peace. It is that same commitment that makes Sri Chinmoy a tireless worker for peace, understanding and tolerance in a dangerous world.” — Russell Barber, WNBC-TV religion editor, Emmy Award winner and producer and host of The First Estate: Religion in Review

My congratulations ... to Sri Chinmoy for his unceasing efforts on behalf of world peace.” — Jean Chrétien, Prime Minister of Canada
